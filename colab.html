COLAB

COLAB is a platform that helps freelancers and impact-driven teams find collaborators who match not just in skills but in working style, values, and communication habits in order to build more meaningful and successful partnerships.

Brief

How might we create a digital solution that helps impact-driven independent workers overcome challenges and build better collaborations?
The user research was realized in a group of four, later the concept and interface design were developed by five group members.

Outcome and key learnings

An app concept that enables users to create meaningful profiles and discover compatible matches based on working style, trust, and collaboration chemistry.
User flow that prioritizes personal values and collaboration chemistry.
Gained confidence in turning user research insights into flows and a mobile prototype in Figma.

The Challenge

How might we create a match-making marketplace for impact-driven work?

Based on desk research, survey analysis, benchmarking and user interviews we defined the key challenges:

  • How can we help collaborators align before deadlines and pressure hit?
  • How might we test-drive collaboration before it’s “too late” to back out?
  • How can onboarding feel smooth, not stressful?

Key learnings

Our users and their needs

What we uncovered was surprisingly consistent: the challenges our target users face weren’t about skills — they were about alignment and communication.

Most platforms focus on skills, portfolios, and availability, while skipping what really drives successful teamwork: shared values, work styles, and communication habits.

The goal was clear: make work chemistry visible, rather than just focusing on credentials.

What makes a collaborator ‘the right fit’?

“It’s not about the quality — it’s about the way they (the collaborators) work.”

The solution

COLAB is designed to support collaboration. It helps users understand their own working style, define what matters most, and find collaborators who align with their values and rhythm.

Goal
Facilitate compatible matches and prevent team clashes later in the project

Methodology
Go beyond traditional CVs and portfolios and focus on values and working styles

Impact
Foster more successful collaborations by anticipating problems

COLAB's compatibility spider

Main user flow and features

1. Setting up the profile

Sharing the working, thinking and collaboration style, not just experience

  • Questions beyond just experience
  • Voice or text answers
  • Connecting personal and professional interests

2. Exploring the community

A space for browsing like-minded freelancers, organisations and events

  • Statistics and information
  • Event cards
  • First glimpse of the community

3. Finding a match

Suggestions based on compatibility and working styles — not just skills

  • Sorted by impact sectors
  • Preview of the collaborator's profile
  • Compatibility spider diagram

4. Connecting over a (virtual) coffee

Reaching out through a shared calendar, testing the vibe, and seeing if it feels like the start of something good

Here the users can:

  • View availability overlap with the match
  • Select coffee date options and specifics
  • Get suggested icebreakers / conversation starters for the meeting
  • View their scheduled events

Arranging a coffee date through the calendar

Future improvements

Learnings

  • It’s not just about skillset, the way people work and connect makes or breaks a collaboration
  • Users need guidance, clear copy and visible interactions are key to good usability
  • We overcomplicated the first version, user testing helped us cut the noise and focus on what really matters
